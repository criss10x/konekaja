Canggu runs on two things that do not get along: crypto income and paper Rupiah. A lot of people here are paid in USDT, hold their savings on-chain, and then hit a wall the moment they need physical cash for something local. This page is about that gap, and how people in Berawa, Batu Bolong, Pererenan and the lanes in between usually close it.

Why cash is awkward in Canggu specifically

Bali is a cash island generally, but Canggu concentrates the problem, because the people here tend to need larger amounts of cash while having the least access to the normal ways of getting it.

The bank account you cannot open

Opening an Indonesian bank account generally requires a KITAS. On a tourist visa or a visa-on-arrival, you usually do not have one, and without a local account the standard P2P route is closed to you: no account to receive the Rupiah into. This is the single biggest reason cash delivery exists as a category here, and it is why the answer to "just use an exchange" so often does not land in Canggu.

Rent that wants cash, in lumps

Villa and long-stay rentals in the Canggu area are frequently settled in cash, and often not monthly, but several months at a time. That turns into a single large Rupiah requirement that no ATM is built to serve. Add the scooter rental deposit, the warungs that never took a card, and the builder or landlord who prefers paper, and the shortfall is not theoretical.

ATM limits that punish exactly this

Many Bali ATMs cap foreign-card withdrawals at roughly Rp 1,500,000 to Rp 3,000,000 per transaction, and the fees are largely fixed per withdrawal rather than proportional. So the more cash you need, the worse the machine gets: you repeat the run, and every fixed charge lands again. We broke the arithmetic down in how nomads avoid sky-high ATM fees in Bali, and Canggu is where that maths bites hardest, because the amounts are big and the trips are frequent.

The short version: ATM fees are mostly fixed, so they hurt most when you need a lot of cash. An OTC spread is a percentage quoted once. The bigger the amount, the more the comparison tips away from the machine.
